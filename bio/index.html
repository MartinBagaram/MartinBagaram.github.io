I was born in northern part of Togo, Kara where I grew up mixing farming life to school. However, I finally completed my high school degree in Sokodé in central part of Togo. From there my adventure started.

I visited couple of West African countries such as Benin and Niger and then was awarded a merit scholarship by both Togolese and Moroccan governments.

My first years of university, I took classes to prepare myself for chemical engineering school. But quickly after few chemistry labs, I was caught back by my passion for the nature and outdoor activities. I therefore switched to forest engineering school. The beauty of the school stems from its combination of military education and a scientific career. I spent a great amount of time in the school since it was a boarding school style. I highly improved myself from the school. I learned leadership, responsibility, and discipline. Few qualities that I proudly brandish.

I graduated from the school with a forest engineering degree and years later I deepened my knowledge by getting a specialized engineering/MSc degree in Waters and Forests specialized in Geo-information of natural resources management. Upon my graduation I worked for a local company operating in GIS and Remote sensing. I worked on assessing natural risks (flood, earthquake, fire, avalanche, etc.) of Arab countries. The work lead to an executive summary appearing on UNISDR website. After 8 months of work I went back to Togo where I worked for a local company specialized in selling ESRI products in Togo. Although the job has the benefit of being up-to-date on the latest technology of ESRI product, it lacked the technical aspect I was looking for and solely focused on marketing and transfer of competence.

I was awarded a fellowship by the European Union, Erasmus Mundus to pursue a master program in Mediterranean Forestry and Multi-objectives decision support systems. I enhanced my knowledge on mathematical modelling known as Research Operations at University of Lisbon (Instituto Superior do Agronomia) . And for the second year of my master program I went to University of Tuscia in Italy. I wrote my master thesis in there on the use of drones - Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) use to assess forest and biodiversity parameters. I obtained my master degree with the maximum points awarded by the Italian system 110/110 e lode which means with honors.

This master opened doors for PhD possibilities explaining my admission at University of Washington, a prestigious university

I co-founded an NGO operating in the environment back in my home country and at University of Washington, I sit on IT commettee board as grad students representative
